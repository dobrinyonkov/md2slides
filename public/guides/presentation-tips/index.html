Presentation Tips for Better Slideshows

Great presentations aren't about fancy slides — they're about clear thinking and good delivery. Here's how to make the most of md2slides.

Structure Your Presentation

Start with a hook Open with a surprising fact, question, or story. The first slide sets the tone.
Use the rule of three Three main points is memorable. More than five and people tune out.
End with a clear call-to-action Tell your audience exactly what you want them to do next.

Slide Design

  • One idea per slide — If a slide needs a paragraph, split it into two
  • Bullet points under 6 words — "Build, measure, learn" not "We need to build the product and then measure the metrics and then learn from the data"
  • Images beat text — A screenshot or diagram communicates faster than a paragraph
  • Consistent formatting — Use heading levels consistently

Markdown Tips for md2slides

  • Use --- on its own line to create a new slide
  • Use ## for slide titles (H1 becomes the presentation title)
  • Use **bold** and *italic* for emphasis
  • Use `code` for inline code or triple backticks for code blocks
  • Lists and sublists work — but keep nesting to one level

Delivery Tips

Practice out loud Reading silently isn't practice. Say it, time it, adjust.
Know your transitions Know what's coming next so you don't pause awkwardly between slides.
Make eye contact You're presenting to humans, not reading bullet points. Look at your audience.
Embrace silence Pausing is powerful. Don't rush to fill every second with words.

Common Mistakes

  • Reading slides word-for-word
  • Too much text on each slide
  • Animations that distract
  • Font sizes too small to read
  • Cramming too much information

Keyboard Shortcuts in md2slides

  • Arrow keys — Navigate slides
  • Escape — Show slide overview
  • F — Fullscreen mode
